Mobile Robotics and Perception Labs

2026

As part of the Mobile Robtoics and Perception course, I completed a three-part project sequence using the TurtleBot 3 Waffle Pi platform, focused on motion planning, mapping, and localization. In the first lab, I implemented RRT and RRT* path planning algorithms in simulation, developing a custom ROS package to generate and visualize paths in Gazebo. The second lab involved real-world calibration and occupancy grid mapping using the robot's LiDAR and IMU sensors, with ROS nodes for sensor fusion and map generation. The final lab focused on localization, where I implemented a particle filter to fuse odometry and LiDAR data for accurate pose estimation, demonstrating successful navigation in both simulated and physical environments.
